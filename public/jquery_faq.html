National Park FAQ

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Michael T. Reynolds is the acting director of the National Park Service.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal: Approximately 22,000 diverse professionals
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park. View the National Park System timeline.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 417 areas covering more than 84 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site? Smallest?
Largest: Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres. Smallest: Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2016: 330,971,689
What is the National Park Service budget?
FY 2017 Enacted: $2.932 billion
What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.
What are concessions?
Concessionaires provide park visitors with lodging, transportation, food services, shops, and other services.

Big Bend

  • 801,163 acres
  • Originally claled Texas Canyons State Park
  • Big Bend National Park is considered to be the largest Chihuahuan Desert protected region in the country.
  • Langford Hot Springs are located in Big Bend National Park. The temperature of the spring is approximately 105 degrees Fahrenheit.

Choke Canyon

  • Paleo Indians crossed the Frio River Valley more than 10,000 years ago
  • Choke Canyon State Park is on the shore of the Choke Canyon Reservoir, which supplies water for Corpus Christi.
  • Latitude: 28.465773 // Longitude: -98.354195
  • Entrance Fees// Adult: $5 Daily // Child 12 Years and Under: Free

Enchanted Rock

  • Around 250,000 people visit the gleaming Enchanted Rock each year
  • Enchanted Rock stands 425 feet above its base, and its highest point is 1,825 feet above sea leve
  • Each dome at the park is part of a 62-square-mile batholith. The majority of the gargantuan granite base is underground
  • It is said to be haunted: While this is just a legend, Tonkawa Indians believed ghosts inhabited the area because creaking sounds came from the dome
